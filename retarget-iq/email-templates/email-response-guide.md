# Email Response Guide - Retarget IQ Voice & Style

## Overall Tone and Voice

### Personality
- **Casual yet professional** - Friendly and approachable, not corporate/stuffy
- **Helpful and solution-oriented** - Focus on solving problems quickly
- **Confident but not arrogant** - Sure of the product, but humble about service
- **Responsive and attentive** - Make customers feel heard and prioritized
- **Ownership-minded** - Take responsibility when things go wrong

### Formality Level
- Use contractions (we'll, you'll, didn't, can't)
- First names only (no Mr./Ms.)
- Exclamation points are okay and common (Hi Jeff!, Thanks!)
- Keep it conversational, not scripted

## Link Sharing Best Practices

### Payment Links
- Always paste full URL, no hyperlinks in template text
- Format: `https://buy.stripe.com/[unique-code]`
- Include on its own line
- Example:
  ```
  Payment Link: https://buy.stripe.com/bJe14oef2fG6ehy6iR4gg0f
  ```

### Video Links
- Always include both hyperlinked title AND thumbnail link
- Format:
  ```
  *Title in Bold with Asterisks* - Watch Video <URL>
  <thumbnail-gif-URL>
  ```
- Include description paragraph after

### API Documentation
- Format: `API Docs - https://docs.retargetiq.com/docs/getting-started`
- Sometimes include the goog_[number] link format (appears to be tracking)

### Login Links
- Always write as: `app.retargetiq.com/login`
- Never as full https:// URL in this context
- Always say "Login link:" before it

## Handling Specific Situations

### When Customer is Frustrated
1. Brief acknowledgment (not over-apologize)
2. Take ownership (Nate's style: "that was on us")
3. Immediate action plan
4. Make it right (credit, push billing date, etc.)
5. Transparency on next steps

Example:
```
Hey [Name].

I wanted to personally reach out and take responsibility here ‚Äî the delay on getting your [thing] fully moving was on us.

That's not the experience we want you to have, especially after signing up, so to make this right we're going to [concrete action].

Our team is actively prioritizing your setup now, and we'll have everything sorted and moving quickly. I'll keep you closely updated over the next couple days so there's full transparency on progress and next steps.

Appreciate your patience, and thank you for trusting us ‚Äî we'll make sure this is handled properly.
```

### When Feature Isn't Available Yet
1. Don't apologize for not having it
2. Confirm it's coming in V2
3. Give specific date (Feb 15th)
4. Explain what they'll be able to do
5. Offer current alternative if available

### When You Don't Know the Answer
1. Don't fake it
2. "I'll get our dev team on this"
3. "I'll loop in [expert]"
4. Set expectation for response time

### When Competitor is Mentioned
1. Don't bash them directly
2. Focus on your data source quality
3. "Fortune 100 and Fortune 500 companies use" line
4. Be confident, not defensive

## Personalization Best Practices

### Use Their Name
- In greeting (always)
- Occasionally in body if it feels natural
- Not in every sentence (that's weird)

### Reference Previous Context
- "As we discussed on the call..."
- "Based on your [use case]..."
- "Since you mentioned [thing]..."

### Connect to Their Use Case
- If they mentioned industry, reference it
- If they mentioned problem, connect solution to it
- Make pricing recommendation based on their needs

### Remember Their Questions
- If they asked multiple things, answer all of them
- Number responses if they numbered questions
- Don't make them ask twice

- "Let me know and I'll shoot over"

## Advanced: Reading Between the Lines

### Urgency Signals from Customer
If they say:
- "I'm ready to move forward" ‚Üí Send payment link immediately
- "Please send me pricing" ‚Üí Use brief pricing template
- "Can you call me?" ‚Üí Coordinate within 24 hours
- "This isn't working" ‚Üí Escalate to dev team, update quickly

### What Different Questions Mean
- "When do I get access?" = I'm about to pay, reassure me
- "What's your data source?" = I'm comparing you to competitors
- "Can I white label?" = I have an agency/reseller model
- "Do you have an API?" = I'm technical, want to automate
- "How many seats?" = I have a team, might need white label

### When to Loop In Team
- Technical bugs ‚Üí Dev team + Alex
- Complex integrations ‚Üí Thomas (he built it)
- High-value deals ‚Üí Loop in Nate
- Payment issues ‚Üí Can usually handle yourself
- Feature requests ‚Üí Note for V2, give timeline
